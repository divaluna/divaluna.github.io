Course Descriptions

Business Technology Management

BTM focuses on combining business courses with information technology in order to provide both aspects to students looking to explore paths in the technology industry. Each semester has given me the opportunity to not only learn the essentials of business and IT, but also to try new courses and to see what will be most useful to me.

summer semester courses description
LAW122: Business Law An introductory course that acts as a prerequisite to further studies of law; examines law in Canada and how it applies to businesses.
ENG510: Gothic Horror A course that analyzes the components of gothic horror and how it exists in popular culture.
ITM820: Information System Security & Privacy A course that examines the technical, operational, and managerial issues of computer and network security in an operational environment.

fall semester courses description
ITM780: Web Design & Management An introductory course to web design that provides the fundamentals to HTML5 and CSS3.
ITM415: Business Process Management A course that introduces modelling, analyzing, and redesigning business process in order to meet target values.
ENG505: Creative Writing An upper year course to build the fundamentals of good writing and developing creative writing abilities.
FIN300: Managerial Finance I An introductory course to major finance concepts like asset valuation, risk assessment, investing. and budgeting.
